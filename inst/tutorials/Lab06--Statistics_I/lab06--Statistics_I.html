Welcome

OK, so, we have focused on descriptive analysis of social networks sofar. Our aim has been to get to know some of the important measures that capture the position of vertices (especially centrality measures) and measures that describe the structure of the network as a whole (such as centralization or component detection), and we spent some time on visualizing networks. Oh, and you have become acquainted with some of the facilities in R do calculate these measures and plot those networks.

From here on out, we are going to change the course of this course :-) We will now move from descriptive analysis to inferential analysis: statistics, that is.

We start that trajectory in this tutorial by looking at graph level measures (including the ones you already learnt about) and will teach you how to statistically test whether the network measure at hand (e.g., its betweenness centralization) is exceptionally high (or low), or whether it is quite average for the sort of network you are considering.

Next, you will learn how to compare networks and even regress a network on several others.

Awesomeness. So, let’s get started.
